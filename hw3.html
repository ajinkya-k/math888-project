**Causal Inference for Networks**

Ajinkya Kokandakar

**Sections** Problem Review ================================================================================ In the [previous section](hw2.html) we discussed the problem setting. Briefly, we are interested in estimating the causal effect of being connected to a pre-specified unit in a social network. In response to the confusion caused due to the use of the term "influential unit" I renamed the term to "focal unit" instead for clarity of exposition. The choice of focal node can be completely arbitrary and does not have to depend on any of the covariates or the network structure recorded in the observations. To review, we observe covariates $\mathbf{X}_i$, outcome $Y_i$ for units $i = 1,2, \dots, n$, and a network $\mathcal{N}$ among the units. We fix a _focal unit_ indexed by $k$, and for each $i \neq k$, we define $A_i = 1$ if units $i$ and $k$ are connected in the network $\mathcal{N}$ and $A_i = 0$ if the units $i$ and $k$ are not connected. We want to estimate: $$ \Delta_k = \mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{N}'}\left[Y_i(1) - Y_i(0)\right] $$ where $\mathcal{N}' = \mathcal{N} \setminus \{k\}$, i.e. the set of all units excluding $k$. Identification ================================================================================ In this section we posit causal assumptions and prove that the estimand of interest is indeed identified. **Assumption**: \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}[Y_i(1) - Y_i(0)] &= \mathbb{E}\left\{\mathbb{E}\left[Y_i(1) - Y_i(0) \mid \mathbf{X}\right] \right\} &&\text{(LIE)}\\[10pt] &= \mathbb{E}\left\{ \mathbb{E}\left[Y_i(1) \mid \mathbf{X}\right] - \mathbb{E}\left[Y_i(0) \mid \mathbf{X}\right] \right\}\\[10pt] &= \mathbb{E}\left\{ \mathbb{E}\left[Y_i(1) \mid \mathbf{X}, A_i = 1\right] - \mathbb{E}\left[Y_i(0) \mid \mathbf{X}, A = 0\right] \right\}\\[10pt] &= \mathbb{E}\left\{ \mathbb{E}\left[Y_i \mid \mathbf{X}, A_i = 1\right] - \mathbb{E}\left[Y_i \mid \mathbf{X}, A = 0\right] \right\}\\ \end{aligned} \]
